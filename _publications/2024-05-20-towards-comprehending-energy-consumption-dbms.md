**Abstract**: In the dynamic landscape of contemporary data-driven technologies, software systems depend heavily on large datasets and continuous data center operations that employ diverse database management systems. The management of vast quantities of data introduces challenges related to energy efficiency, making the selection of energy-efficient ("green") database systems increasingly important. To address a gap in the software engineering literature on DBMS energy usage, we present DBJoules, a tool to measure database energy consumption, and report an empirical study comparing the energy profiles of popular systems (MySQL, PostgreSQL, MongoDB, and Couchbase) under realistic workloads. Our results characterize how query types and system configurations influence energy use and highlight opportunities for optimizing DBMS energy efficiency.
